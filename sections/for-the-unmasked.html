Project Overview

For The Unmasked. You kill all the masked overlords using the same masks that they wield to free the unmasked ones from their mind control.

This Project was initially made in 48 hours but it is back in development as we plan to take all of the feedback given to us from the game jam and create a full fledged demo of our original ambitious idea. I will write what we already have in the released version of the game down below. Though The game is taking a pivot into making sure that we expand on what made the game enjoyable based on the players' feedback

Key Features

Ability Based Combat

For The Unmasked features a feedback driven combat system where the player can use the masks of the enemies they defeat to gain new abilities.

Level Design

With very limited time, the level was designed to be non-linear and provide multiple paths for the player to take. Based on the player's choice and their available abilities, different paths will be more or less effective.

Managing The Scope

With a team of 6 and a very small window of 48 hours, the scope of the game had to be managed carefully. It's a fairly large team for a game jam, so we had to make sure everyone had something to do and that we weren't biting off more than we could chew.

The Design Process

Combat Design

The combat was designed to be fast paced and fluid. The player can switch between masks instantly, allowing for quick combos and attacks using the different kits the masks provide.

The player has a base kit which is shared across everything, from grunts to bosses and even the player.

I designed the combat with very tight instructions given to the animator for the attack timings to ensure that the combat felt snappy and responsive.

Level Design

With only 48 hours to design and build the game, the level had to be designed with very tight constraints. Working with an agile system I quickly prototyped the level using basic shapes to test the flow and pacing of the game. Then proceeded with dressing up the level with the time I had left.

The level was designed to be non-linear and provide multiple paths for the player to take. Based on the player's choice and their available abilities, different paths will be more or less effective. That way theres some level of replayability and player agency. As we work more on the game this objective will be expanded on more to make sure that replayability and player agency are at the forefront of the level design.

Working With The Team & Managing The Scope

When working with 6 (2 Programmers, 1 Designer/Developer, 2 3D Animators/Artists, 1 2D Artist) people on a game jam, it's important to make sure that everyone has something to do and that we're not biting off more than we can chew. With most of us working together for the first time we needed to make sure that we were all on the same page and that we were able to communicate effectively.

I took on the role of team lead and was responsible for making sure that everything was documented and communication was seamless within the team. With the time constraint of 48 hours, having as much details for each task as possible was crucial to making sure that we didnt have to make multiple revisions for the same task.
